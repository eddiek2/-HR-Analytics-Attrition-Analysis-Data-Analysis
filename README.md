# HR Analytics Employee Attrition Analysis

## Project Overview
This project analyzes employee attrition patterns using the Kaggle HR Analytics Case Study dataset. The goal is to identify key factors that drive employees to leave the organization using Google Sheets pivot tables and charts.

## Tools Used
- Google Sheets (Pivot Tables, Charts, IFS formula)

## Dataset
- **Source:** [HR Analytics Case Study by Vijay Choudhary](https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/vjchoudhary7/hr-analytics-case-study)

## Spreadsheet
[View the Google Sheets workbook here](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/19G9kR8PraLiv2ZTtyY_fKbMO29x5XJsSLneVmDpiGnI/edit?usp=sharing)
- **Records:** 4,410 employees
- **File used:** general_data.csv

## Key Findings

### 1. Attrition by Department
Human Resources has the highest attrition rate at 30.2%, nearly double Research & Development (15.7%) and Sales (15.0%), despite being the smallest department.

### 2. Average Monthly Income by Department
HR also has the lowest average monthly income ($57,904) while R&D has the highest ($67,188), suggesting a possible link between compensation and attrition.

### 3. Attrition by Business Travel
Attrition rate roughly triples as travel frequency increases from 8.0% (Non-Travel) to 15.0% (Travel_Rarely) to 24.9% (Travel_Frequently).

### 4. Attrition by Gender
Minimal gender difference Female: 15.3%, Male: 16.7%. Gender is not a strong attrition driver in this dataset.

### 5. Attrition by Marital Status
Single employees leave at 25.5%, more than double the rate of Divorced (10.1%) and Married (12.5%) employees.

### 6. Attrition by Job Role
Research Directors have the highest attrition rate (23.75%), followed by Research Scientists (18.15%) and Sales Executives (16.87%). Manufacturing Directors show the lowest rate (11.03%).

### 7. Average Years at Company by Attrition
Employees who left averaged 5.1 years at the company, compared to 7.4 years for those who stayed.

### 8. Attrition by Income Band
Lower and mid income employees (Low: 16.4%, Medium: 17.6%) show higher attrition than higher earners (High: 13.4%, Very High: 13.9%).
